## Topic 1 · Korean Cafe Culture: More Than Just Coffee

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

In South Korea, it is almost impossible to walk down a street without seeing a cafe. However, Korean cafes are not just places to grab a quick cup of coffee. For many people, a cafe is a second home, an office, or a library. Have you ever heard the word 'Cagongjok'? It means people who study or work at cafes. Many adults and students pack their laptops and head to places like Starbucks to concentrate on their work.

Why do they do this instead of staying home? Interestingly, a completely quiet room can sometimes make people feel sleepy or easily distracted. On the other hand, the gentle background noise of a cafe—like people talking softly, the sound of the coffee machine, and smooth music—actually helps them focus better. This is called 'white noise'. Also, being around other hardworking people can give you the motivation to work hard, too.

Of course, some cafe owners worry about customers who stay for too long after buying only one cheap drink. But overall, this unique cafe culture has become a very natural and important part of modern Korean life, offering a comfortable space to escape from our busy daily routines.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions

[Key Vocabulary & Expressions]
* grab a cup of coffee: 커피를 (간단히/빨리) 마시다
* concentrate on: ~에 집중하다
* distracted: 산만한, 주의가 산만해진
* white noise: 백색 소음 (집중을 돕는 일정한 배경 소음)
* motivation: 동기 부여

[Pronunciation Tips]
* concentrate [ˈkɑːnsəntreɪt]: '콘센트레이트'가 아니라 앞부분 '칸'에 강세를 두고 길게 발음하세요. (칸-슨트레이트)
* comfortable [ˈkʌmftərbəl]: '컴포터블'이 아니라 중간 o 발음을 거의 내지 않고 '컴프터블'에 가깝게 부드럽게 발음합니다.

## Topic 2 · The Future of Autonomous Driving

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

Imagine getting into your car in the morning, holding a cup of coffee, and reading the news while the car drives itself to your office. This might sound like a scene from a science fiction movie, but the era of "autonomous driving" is arriving faster than we think.

Today, many tech and car companies are developing advanced systems that allow vehicles to navigate roads without human drivers. These self-driving cars use powerful computers, cameras, and sensors to understand their surroundings. They can stop at red lights, safely avoid walking people, and even change lanes on a busy highway.

The biggest advantage of this technology is safety. Because computers do not get tired, look at their smartphones, or drive after drinking, experts believe autonomous cars could prevent thousands of accidents. Also, it can give elderly people or those with physical disabilities the freedom to travel easily.

However, we still have some big questions to answer. What happens if the car's computer makes a mistake? Who is responsible for a car accident? Furthermore, companies must work hard to build trust so that every customer feels completely safe letting a machine take the wheel. While it may take some time before we can completely let go of the steering wheel, the way we travel is definitely changing.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions
* autonomous driving: 자율 주행 (self-driving 과 같은 뜻으로 쓰임)
* navigate: 길을 찾다, 주행하다
* surroundings: 주변 환경
* advantage: 장점, 이점
* take the wheel: 운전대를 잡다 (책임지다라는 비유적 표현으로도 쓰임)

[Pronunciation Tips (주의할 발음)]
autonomous [ɔːˈtɑːnəməs]: '오토노머스'가 아니라 '어-타-너머스'에 가깝습니다. '타' 부분에 강세를 줍니다.
vehicle [ˈviːəkl]: '비히클'에서 h 소리가 거의 묵음 처리되어 '비어클'처럼 부드럽게 발음됩니다.
