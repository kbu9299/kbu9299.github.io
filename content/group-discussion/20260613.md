## Topic 1 · Article 1: Summer Nights at Hangang Park

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

Summer is officially here, and the weather is getting hot and humid in South Korea. During this season, one of the most popular places to visit in Seoul is the Hangang (Han River) Park. When the sun goes down and the evening breeze blows, thousands of citizens visit the riverside to relax after a long day of work.

People bring picnic mats, small tents, and portable fans to sit on the green grass. What do they do there? Mostly, they eat! The absolute favorite food is "Chimaek," which is a combination of fried chicken and cold beer. Delivery riders even bring the food right to your picnic mat. Also, cooking instant ramen using special machines at convenience stores is a must-do activity.

Recently, the parks have offered more than just food. You can enjoy free music festivals, beautiful water fountain shows, and amazing drone light shows in the night sky. While the city gets busy and stressful during the day, the Hangang Park transforms into a massive, peaceful playground for families, couples, and friends every summer night.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions
* humid: 습한
* breeze: 산들바람
* portable: 휴대용의, 들고 다닐 수 있는
* combination: 조합
* transform: 변모하다, 완전히 바뀌다

## Topic 2 · The Expansion of the Subscription Economy

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

The global market is experiencing a massive shift towards the "Subscription Economy." Traditionally, consumers purchased goods to own them permanently. Today, however, people increasingly prefer paying a recurring monthly fee to simply access products and services. This revolutionary trend started with digital media, such as Netflix for movies and YouTube Premium for videos without ads.

Now, the subscription model has expanded into almost every aspect of daily life. You can subscribe to receive fresh coffee beans, organic pet food, daily vitamins, and even luxury cars. For companies, this business model is incredibly attractive because it guarantees a predictable revenue stream and builds long-term relationships with their customers. For consumers, it offers unparalleled convenience and the illusion of saving money.

However, there is a hidden downside: "subscription fatigue." Many adults in their 40s and 50s are slowly realizing they are paying for multiple services they rarely use. Automatic payments silently drain bank accounts each month, making it easy to forget what we signed up for. Financial experts strongly advise us to audit our subscription lists every few months to cancel unnecessary auto-renewals. While convenience is great, managing our digital expenses has become a new necessary skill.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions
* recurring: 반복되는, 되풀이되는
* predictable: 예측 가능한
* unparalleled: 비할 데 없는, 견줄 데 없는 (아주 뛰어난)
* fatigue: 피로, 피로감
* audit: 회계 감사, (자신의 지출이나 내역을) 면밀히 검사하다

### {{< icon note >}} Pronunciation Tips
* recurring [rɪˈkɜːrɪŋ]: '리커링'에서 '커'에 강세를 주어 '리-커-링'으로 발음합니다.
* unparalleled [ʌnˈpærəleld]: 다소 길고 까다로운 단어입니다. '패'에 강세를 주어 '언-패-럴-렐드'로 발음합니다.
