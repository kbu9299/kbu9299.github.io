## Topic 1 · Article 1: The Culture of "Jangma" in South Korea

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

Every summer, South Korea experiences a long rainy season called "Jangma." It usually starts in late June and lasts for about a month. During this time, the sky is often dark, the air is very humid, and sudden heavy rain can cause traffic jams and floods in the city. Many people feel tired and depressed because they cannot see the bright sun.

However, Jangma also brings a very unique and delicious culture to Korea. When it rains, Korean people naturally think about two things: "Pajeon" (green onion pancake) and "Makgeolli" (traditional rice wine). You will see many traditional restaurants full of people enjoying this combination on rainy evenings. Some people say that the sound of frying the pancake in hot oil sounds exactly like the sound of rain falling on the ground.

Also, the delivery industry changes during Jangma. Because people do not want to go outside, the demand for food delivery goes up. But the heavy rain makes driving dangerous for motorcycle riders. Therefore, it is a time when we need to be more patient and show warm understanding to the delivery workers. Jangma is definitely a challenging season, but it is also a time to slow down, listen to the rain, and enjoy warm food with good friends.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions
* humid: 습한
* traffic jam: 교통 체증
* combination: 조합, 결합
* demand: 수요, 요구
* patient: 참을성 있는, 인내심 있는

## Topic 2 · The Shift Towards Sustainable Fashion

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

For the past two decades, the global apparel industry has been dominated by "Fast Fashion." Brands produced massive quantities of trendy, inexpensive clothing, encouraging consumers to buy outfits, wear them a few times, and quickly throw them away. While this business model was incredibly profitable, it created a devastating environmental crisis. The fashion industry is currently responsible for nearly 10% of global carbon emissions and produces mountains of textile waste that end up in landfills.

Fortunately, a significant paradigm shift is underway. A growing movement called "Sustainable Fashion" is challenging this wasteful culture. Modern consumers, particularly those in their 40s and 50s who value quality and legacy, are increasingly demanding transparency from clothing brands. They want to know if the cotton was farmed ethically, if the workers were paid fair wages, and if the materials can be recycled.

In response, innovative companies are completely redesigning their supply chains. They are utilizing recycled plastics to make winter jackets, introducing vegan leather made from mushrooms, and offering repair services to extend the lifespan of their garments. Furthermore, the second-hand clothing market is experiencing explosive growth. This transition proves that looking stylish does not have to come at the expense of our planet. The future of fashion is no longer about consuming more; it is about choosing better.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions
* devastating: 파괴적인, 엄청난 손상을 가하는
* landfill: 쓰레기 매립지
* transparency: 투명성
* ethically: 윤리적으로
* extend the lifespan: 수명을 연장하다

### {{< icon note >}} Pronunciation Tips
