## Topic 1 · The Trot Music Craze and "Filial Piety" Ticketing

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

f you turn on the television in South Korea today, you will easily find programs about "Trot" music. Trot is a traditional Korean pop music genre. In the past, young people thought Trot was old-fashioned. However, recently, young and handsome singers like Lim Young-woong have made this music incredibly popular again, especially among adults in their 50s, 60s, and 70s.

For many older adults, these singers are like superstars. They join fan clubs, wear matching colored T-shirts, and stream their favorite singer's songs all day. This brings them a lot of joy and energy in their daily lives.

Because of this massive popularity, getting a concert ticket is extremely difficult. This has created a new culture called "Hyodo (Filial Piety) Ticketing." Sons and daughters in their 20s and 30s sit in front of fast computers, clicking their mice as fast as they can to buy concert tickets for their parents. If they succeed, they become the best son or daughter in the world. It is a beautiful and funny way that modern music connects different generations in a family.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions
* old-fashioned: 구식의, 옛날 유행의
* filial piety: 효도
* massive: 거대한, 엄청난

## Topic 2 · The Importance of the "Third Place" for Modern Adults

### {{< icon book >}} Reading

In modern sociology, there is a fascinating concept known as the "Third Place." According to this theory, your home is your "First Place," dedicated to family and rest. Your workplace or office is your "Second Place," focused on duty, professional performance, and stress. The "Third Place" is an entirely different environment: a neutral, welcoming community space—such as a local cafe, an independent bookstore, a public park, or a neighborhood pub.

For adults navigating their 40s and 50s, establishing a reliable third place is crucial for maintaining mental health and preventing burnout. These spaces offer a temporary escape from domestic responsibilities and professional pressures. In a third place, you do not have to be a parent, a spouse, or a manager; you can simply be yourself. It is a setting where spontaneous conversations happen, new ideas are born, and a sense of belonging is cultivated without any strict obligations.

However, in today's highly digitalized and fast-paced society, genuine third places are slowly disappearing. People are increasingly spending their free time isolated at home, endlessly scrolling through smartphones. Psychologists warn that this loss of physical community spaces is a leading cause of urban loneliness. Actively seeking out and cherishing a physical space to unwind and connect with others is no longer a luxury, but a fundamental necessity for a balanced life.

### {{< icon note >}} Key Vocabulary & Expressions

* spontaneous: 자발적인, 자연스럽게 일어나는
* obligation: 의무, 책임
